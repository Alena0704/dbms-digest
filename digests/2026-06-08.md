# DBMS Weekly — 2026-06-14 (week of 2026-06-08–2026-06-14)

_18 items · sources scanned: ~30 · filtered out as marketing/ads: 8_

## PostgreSQL
- **How the Other Half Counts** — Christophe Pettus surveys how Oracle, Db2, MySQL, SQLite, DuckDB, and Snowflake gather planner statistics vs. PostgreSQL's sampling `ANALYZE`; the real axis turns out to be stats freshness and who owns it, not the join algorithm. [link](https://thebuild.com/blog/how-the-other-half-counts/)
- **All Your GUCs in a Row: deadlock_timeout** — clarifies that `deadlock_timeout` sets how *often* PostgreSQL checks for a deadlock, not how long a deadlock is tolerated; part of a near-daily GUC-internals series. [link](https://thebuild.com/blog/all-your-gucs-in-a-row-deadlocktimeout/)
- **All Your GUCs in a Row: DateStyle** — on why `DateStyle` conflates two unrelated behaviours (input parsing and output formatting) in a single GUC. [link](https://thebuild.com/blog/all-your-gucs-in-a-row-datestyle/)
- **All Your GUCs in a Row: data_sync_retry** — the GUC born from the Postgres fsync-reliability durability saga, and why the default is what it is. [link](https://thebuild.com/blog/all-your-gucs-in-a-row-datasyncretry/)
- **My Three Top PostgreSQL 19 Features** — Stefanie Janine walks through `INSERT ... ON CONFLICT DO SELECT`, the new in-core `REPACK` (with `CONCURRENTLY`), and SQL/PGQ graph queries landing in PG19. [link](https://proopensource.it/blog/postgresql-19-features)
- **PostgreSQL Anonymizer 3.1: local differential privacy** — Dalibo's data-masking extension adds local-differential-privacy masking techniques (announcement light on detail; verify changelog before relying on it). [link](https://www.postgresql.org/about/news/postgresql-anonymizer-31-introducing-local-differential-privacy-3311/)

## PostgreSQL mailing lists
- **[hackers] FK fast-path: OOB write + missed FK checks during batched RI checks** — three defects in PG19's new batched foreign-key fast path (an out-of-bounds write and two paths silently committing FK-violating orphan rows); reproducers confirmed, Amit Langote pushed the fix and it's on the PG19 Open Items list. Data-integrity regression in a just-shipped beta feature. [link](https://www.postgresql.org/message-id/flat/CAM527d_2OpJ3KCOT1QqGh4neCPpgZTgM%2BVUxTqVgOSweOzTDQw%40mail.gmail.com) `[committed]`
- **[hackers] Commit Sequence Numbers and Visibility (CSN snapshots)** — Jeff Davis proposes concrete rules for CSN-based snapshot acquisition; Matthias van de Meent pushes back that coupling snapshot acquisition to durability would impose synchronous-flush waits on readers, counter-proposing separate durability-horizon WAL records. Core architectural debate for a future MVCC redesign. [link](https://www.postgresql.org/message-id/flat/CAEze2WihVmsjrEdMXmqhmsy-R8puBjopuvpqmHYwFP_usF%2BaSw%40mail.gmail.com) `[open]`
- **[hackers] RFC: a constraint-enforcement layer for cross-partition uniqueness** — Greg Burd reframes the perennial "global unique index on partitioned tables" problem as a separate `pg_constraint_method` abstraction (a lightweight WAL-logged membership map) instead of a full global index, citing prior global-index attempts. [link](https://www.mail-archive.com/pgsql-hackers%40postgresql.org/msg230264.html) `[open]`
- **[hackers] Any JIT-compiled query crashes (SIGILL) on libLLVM 19 + ASAN CI build** — cfbot failures blamed on the Row Pattern Recognition patch turn out to be a feature-independent crash: on the ASAN+LLVM 19.1 config any JIT-compiled query SIGILLs on plain master; minimal repro and regression test posted. Was masking real CI signal on in-flight patches. [link](https://www.mail-archive.com/pgsql-hackers%40postgresql.org/msg230280.html) `[patch posted]`

## Wider DBMS & distributed data
- **The only scalable delete in Postgres is DROP TABLE** — PlanetScale argues that under MVCC, bulk `DELETE` doesn't cheaply reclaim space (dead tuples, VACUUM, bloat), so partition-drop / `DROP TABLE` is the only delete that scales; lively HN discussion. [link](https://planetscale.com/blog/the-only-scalable-delete)
- **Our MongoDB TLA+ workshop** — Murat Demirbas reports an order-of-magnitude better onboarding when LLM assistance removes TLA+ syntax as the barrier, letting engineers formally model real replication/storage subsystems hands-on; includes a distilled "AI-assisted TLA+ best practices" guide. [link](https://muratbuffalo.blogspot.com/2026/06/our-mongodb-tla-workshop.html)
- **Postgres by Example** — example-driven Postgres reference repo that surfaced on HN; useful as a teaching/cheat-sheet resource. [link](https://github.com/boringcollege/postgres-by-example)

## Commercial engines (SQL Server, Oracle, MySQL, …)
- **RUM: storing more in the index** — Franck Pachot deep-dive on Postgres RUM indexes (carrying extra payload in the index to serve ESR-style equality/sort/range queries the way other engines' covering indexes do). [link](https://dev.to/franckpachot/rum-storing-more-in-the-index-4hoe)
- **GIN: understanding PostgreSQL's inverted index and its limitations** — Pachot walks the internals and trade-offs of GIN indexing, with cross-engine framing. [link](https://dev.to/franckpachot/gin-understanding-postgresqls-inverted-index-and-its-limitations-40hn)

## Research & cutting edge
- **Multiversion Concurrency Control for Multiversion B-Trees** — cMVBT redesigns the multiversion B-tree with optimistic latches for writers and latch-free range scans plus continuous GC, beating version-chain MVCC on write throughput and arbitrary-version range scans. [link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.09133)  _[paper]_
- **Bespoke-Card: synthesizing workload-specific cardinality estimators** — generate a per-workload cardinality estimator instead of tuning one generic model (AIDB@VLDB'26). [link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.09361)  _[paper]_
- **Determination Provenance: From Ambiguity to Algebra** — Joseph M. Hellerstein gives an algebraic treatment of determination/provenance. [link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.10270)  _[paper]_
