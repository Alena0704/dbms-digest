## PostgreSQL
- **How soon is `now()`? A frozen-timestamp retry-loop bug** — Oskar Dudycz walks through a real distributed-locking bug: `now()` returns the *transaction* start time, so a retry loop sharing one transaction kept re-evaluating `last_updated < now() - timeout` against a constant and never made progress; fix is `clock_timestamp()`. Includes the testing-seam lesson on why unit + e2e tests both missed it. [link](https://event-driven.io/en/how-soon-is-now-in-postgresql/)
- **5 PostgreSQL locking behaviours that trip people up** — Shinya Kato (PostgreSQL engineer, NTT) on counterintuitive production-outage locks: FIFO lock-queue chained blocking behind a waiting `ACCESS EXCLUSIVE`, invisible FK `FOR KEY SHARE` deadlocks, unique-check INSERT deadlocks, the wraparound-prevention autovacuum that won't be cancelled, and VACUUM's hidden truncate-phase `ACCESS EXCLUSIVE` (and how it forcibly cancels standby SELECTs). [link](https://dev.to/shinyakato_/5-postgresql-locking-behaviors-that-trip-people-up-4k7n)
- **PostgreSQL Anonymizer 3.1 — Local Differential Privacy + critical CVE** — Dalibo adds a formally-bounded LDP masking mechanism (Generalized Randomized Response, epsilon-tunable) for survey/categorical data, and fixes CVE-2026-9617, a privilege-escalation-to-superuser flaw (high risk on PG 14 and clusters upgraded from ≤14). Upgrade urged; SQL workaround posted. [link](https://www.postgresql.org/about/news/postgresql-anonymizer-31-introducing-local-differential-privacy-3311/)

## PostgreSQL conferences (PGConf.dev 2026 trip reports)
- **PGConf.dev 2026: HA cluster-topology, wait-events, collation** — Jeremy Schneider's trip summary surfaces real design directions discussed in Vancouver: Postgres lacks a first-class "cluster/topology" concept (a `pg_nodes`-style view, promotion-candidate discovery on standbys); Sami Imseih's pg_stat_statements LWLock/query-text-file concurrency patches; per-wait-event counters; and the unsolved ICU-upgrade-invalidates-indexes collation problem (with Oracle's pragmatic per-operator default as a reference). [link](https://ardentperf.com/2026/05/26/pgconf-dev-2026-trip-summary/)
- **PGConf.dev 2026 reflections — SQL/PGQ, multithreading, resize-shared-buffers** — Ashutosh Bapat reports on the graph-DB developer meeting (SQL/PGQ + AGE), the logical-replication working group (DDL replication and gaps before LR is usable for high-volume OLAP), the multithreading effort, and a quick design agreement with Databricks engineers (Heikki et al.) on resizing shared buffers without restart, built on the PG 19 shared-memory rework. [link](https://ashutoshpg.blogspot.com/2026/05/my-reflections-on-pgconfdev-2026.html)
